The Great Wall of Benin
The Great Wall of Benin or "Iya" in the Edo language.

Iya was "Four times longer than the Great Wall of China, and consumed a hundred times more material than the Great Pyramid of Cheops" [4] [5]

"The people of multitude"

People of the Benin Kingdom were called Ubini, "Ile-Ibinu", derived from the word Ubi, which means multitude. Ubi-ni means ”the people of multitude.”

Around the 15th century, Portuguese merchants who conducted trade with the people of ”Ile-Ibinu” coined the name ”Bini” or ”Benin,” as it is called today.

Dynasties of Benin, 1200AD - Present
The Edo people of Southern Nigeria founded Benin. They no longer wanted to be ruled by their kings, known as the Ogisos. They asked Ekaladerhan, a prince from Ife to take control. The first Oba, or king, in Benin was Eweka. He was the son of the prince from Ife.
It was not until the 15th century during the reign of Oba Ewuare the Great that the kingdom's administrative centre, the city Ubinu, began to be known as Benin City by the Portuguese, later be adopted by the locals as well.
