One of the Oldest Surviving Kingdoms in the World is the Great Benin Kingdom dating back to 40BCE. The walls of Benin City and its surrounding kingdom were a man-made wonder. Described as “the world's largest earthworks prior to the mechanical era”.

"The people of multitude"

People of the Benin Kingdom were called Ubini, "Ile-Ibinu", derived from the word Ubi, which means multitude. Ubi-ni means ”the people of multitude.”

Around the 15th century, Portuguese merchants who conducted trade with the people of ”Ile-Ibinu” coined the name ”Bini” or ”Benin,” as it is called today.

The Great Wall of Benin
The Great Wall of Benin or "Iya" in the Edo language.

Iya was "Four times longer than the Great Wall of China, and consumed a hundred time more material than the Great Pyramid of Cheops" [1] [2]

  • The Benins are the best bronze casters (Igun – eronmwon) in the world

  • The first palace in Benin Kingdom was located at Ugbekun quarters built by Ogiso Igodo in about 40-42BC

    The city and its surrounding villages were purposely laid out to form perfect fractals, with similar shapes repeated in the rooms of each house, and the house itself, and the clusters of houses in the village in mathematically predictable patterns.
